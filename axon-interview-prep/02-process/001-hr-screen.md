# HR Screen

## Goal

Confirm motivation, communication, availability, salary range, English ability, and role fit.

## Questions To Prepare

1. Tell me about yourself.
2. Why Axon?
3. Why this Junior Software Engineer role?
4. What do you know about Axon?
5. Which Axon product interests you most, and why?
6. What are your strengths?
7. What are your weaknesses, and how are you improving them?
8. How do you use AI in your work?
9. When should you avoid relying too much on AI?
10. Tell me about a project you are proud of.
11. Tell me about a time you had conflict or disagreement with a teammate.
12. What kind of team or engineering culture are you looking for?
13. Are you comfortable with the hybrid schedule in Ho Chi Minh City?
14. What are your salary expectations?
15. When can you start?
16. Do you have any questions for me?

## Strong Message

> I am looking for a role where I can grow quickly while contributing to meaningful production systems. Axon is attractive to me because the mission is clear and important, and the role combines backend engineering, collaboration, code quality, and AI-assisted development. I am comfortable with the hybrid schedule because I value in-person collaboration, especially as an engineer who wants to learn from experienced teammates.

## Axon Product Angle

Prepare one favorite product and a clear reason. A strong option is Draft One:

> The Axon product I am most interested in is Draft One because it uses AI to reduce report-writing time for officers, which can let them spend more time on field work and community interaction. I also think it is technically interesting because the product must balance productivity with accuracy, privacy, reviewability, and accountability.

Other products you can mention:

- Body cameras: strong angle for reliability, evidence quality, and real-world safety.
- Evidence.com: strong angle for secure storage, access control, auditability, and case workflows.
- Axon Fleet: strong angle for video capture, operational workflows, and high-pressure usage.

## English Speaking Tips

- Speak slower than usual.
- Use short, clear sentences.
- Structure answers: context, action, result.
- Ask for clarification when needed.

Useful phrase:

> Could I take a few seconds to organize my thoughts?
