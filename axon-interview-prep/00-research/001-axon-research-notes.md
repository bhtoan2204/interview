## Official Hiring Signals From Axon

Axon's official "Getting Hired" page recommends that candidates:

- Research Axon product offerings and understand how the technology is applied.
- Identify one product or service that resonates with them.
- Prepare a story showing how their background intersects with Axon's solutions.
- Research interviewers when names are available.
- Understand Axon's values.
- Review their own background and anticipate where interviewers may dig deeper.
- Prepare examples showing how they applied feedback to deliver outcomes.
- Ask thoughtful questions because interviews go both ways.

## Axon Values To Map Into Answers

Axon's culture page lists these values:

- Be Obsessed
- Aim Far
- Win Right
- Own It
- Join Forces
- Expect Candor
- Boldly Go

## How To Map Your Experience To Axon Values

### Be Obsessed

Use stories about customer impact, production quality, and reliability.

Example:

> In payment and order systems, small errors can affect many real users, so I pay close attention to correctness, idempotency, reconciliation, and observability.

### Aim Far

Use migration and architecture stories.

Example:

> Rebuilding legacy PHP services into Go and migrating 30M+ records was not just a short-term fix. It created a cleaner foundation for future scalability and reliability.

### Win Right

Use security, privacy, and auditability.

Example:

> I worked on masking and encryption for sensitive customer data across MySQL, Elasticsearch, and Redis, because shipping quickly should not come at the cost of privacy or safety.

### Own It

Use payment ownership.

Example:

> I independently designed and implemented POS/online payment flows, then took responsibility for reliability, audit trail compliance, and production behavior.

### Join Forces

Use collaboration with Product, Design, QA, DevOps, or other backend engineers.

Example:

> Payment flows required coordination across backend, operations, third-party providers, and accounting rules, so I focused on clear contracts, state definitions, and validation.

### Expect Candor

Use code review and feedback.

Example:

> I try to make technical tradeoffs explicit during design discussions. If I disagree, I explain the risk and listen to the reasoning from teammates.

### Boldly Go

Use AI-assisted development, new technical areas, and complex integrations.

Example:

> I am comfortable learning new domains quickly, such as aviation workflows, payment providers, CDC migration, and observability tools.

## Likely Interviewer Direction

Based on Axon's official hiring page and the job description, interviewers may ask questions that test:

- Whether you understand the mission.
- Whether you can connect your backend experience to public safety impact.
- Whether you can own features while receiving mentorship.
- Whether you can validate AI-generated code.
- Whether you can explain production incidents and reliability decisions.
- Whether your behavior matches Axon's values.
